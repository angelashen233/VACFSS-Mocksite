About VACFSS

An urban Indigenous Child and Family Services Agency, strengthening families through restorative, culturally grounded care since 1988.

Who We Are

An Indigenous-Led, Indigenous-Governed Agency

VACFSS is an urban Indigenous Child and Family Services Agency (ICFSA) operating on the unceded and ancestral territories of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam), Sḵwx̱wú7mesh (Squamish), and səlilwətaɬ (Tsleil-Waututh) Nations. We deliver services through Delegation Agreements with the Provincial Director of Child Welfare — but our governance and accountability remain independent. Our practice is grounded in restorative Indigenous child welfare, with internal policies that exceed the standards set by the provincial government.

  • Independent Indigenous governance and accountability
  • Restorative, least-disruptive child welfare practice
  • Internal policies that exceed provincial (CFCSA) standards
  • Full delegation for child protection since April 2008

Our History

Decades of Growth & Commitment

From a small grassroots society to a fully delegated Indigenous child welfare agency, our history reflects a steady journey toward Indigenous-led care.

June 1988

Established as the Mamele Benevolent Society, with a mandate to develop Indigenous child welfare and in-home support programs for urban Indigenous families.

1992

Reincorporated as the Vancouver Aboriginal Child and Family Services Society, delivering family support services, advocacy, cultural support for children in care, and cultural awareness training for caregivers.

1996

Achieved designation status following community consultations, enabling notification to First Nations when Indigenous children were removed from families in the Lower Mainland.

1999

Launched the Guardianship Pilot Project and began negotiations with the Ministry for delegated child welfare services.

December 2001

Signed the Delegation Enabling Agreement (DEA), authorizing VACFSS to deliver delegated Resource and Guardianship services.

2002 – 2004

Guardianship services established (2002), Resources services launched (2003), and Family Support evolved into Family Preservation and Reunification (2004).

April 2008

Received full child protection function transfer from MCFD — achieving full delegation as an Indigenous Child and Family Services Agency.

Our Symbol

The Story Behind Our Logo

Our logo was designed by Nisga'a artist Laurence Wilson in 1993. It translates to "Human and Wolf Child" — symbolizing our mission to strengthen Indigenous families and protect the spirit of every child in our care. The wolf represents loyalty, family, and community — values that remain at the heart of everything VACFSS does.
