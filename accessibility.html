Accessibility Statement

Accessibility

VACFSS is committed to ensuring this website is accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities, so that all families, caregivers, and community members can find the support they need.

Our Commitment

Building an Inclusive Website

We believe that everyone — regardless of ability, device, or connection — should be able to access information about our programs, find support during a crisis, or apply to become a caregiver. We continually work to improve the usability and accessibility of this website for all visitors.

Standard We Aim For

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with a wide range of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological disabilities.

What We've Built In

Accessibility Measures on This Site

This website includes the following accessibility features:

  • Keyboard NavigationEvery page can be navigated using a keyboard alone, including a "Skip to main content" link on every page.
  • Descriptive Alt TextMeaningful images include descriptive alternative text; decorative icons are hidden from screen readers.
  • Colour ContrastText and background colour combinations are designed to meet readable contrast levels, including the high-contrast redesign of our contact section.
  • Readable TypographyBody text uses a comfortable minimum font size and line height, and scales fluidly across screen sizes.
  • Responsive DesignPages adapt across mobile, tablet, and desktop, with a dedicated mobile navigation menu.
  • Semantic StructurePages use semantic landmarks (header, nav, main, footer) and proper heading levels to support screen readers.
  • Labelled FormsAll form fields have associated labels, and required fields are clearly indicated.
  • Visible Focus StatesInteractive elements display a clear focus indicator for keyboard users.
Known Limitations

Ongoing Work

Despite our best efforts, some content may not yet be fully accessible. This can include PDF reports and publications, and content hosted on third-party platforms outside our direct control, such as the VACFSS Careers portal. We are working to improve these areas over time and welcome your feedback.

Let Us Know

If you encounter an accessibility barrier on this website, or have suggestions for how we can improve, please get in touch. We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within a reasonable timeframe.
