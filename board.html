Governance

Board of Directors

Guided by Indigenous leadership and community wisdom, our volunteer Board of Directors provides governance and direction rooted in culture, accountability, and care.

Leadership

A Board Rooted in Community

The VACFSS Board of Directors brings a combined 45 years of volunteer, leadership, and community service to the Society. Drawn from Nations and communities across the province and beyond, our directors guide VACFSS with deep cultural knowledge, professional expertise, and an unwavering commitment to Indigenous children, youth, and families.

Meet the Board

Our Directors

Linda Stiller
Chair
Williams Lake Indian Band, Shuswap Nation

Linda joined the VACFSS board in 2010 and has served as Chair since 2012. She holds a social work degree from McMaster University (1976) and is a former BC child protection worker, later retiring from federal service managing First Nations child and family programs.

Madeleine MacIvor
Co-Chair
Métis, Lac Ste Anne, Alberta

Madeleine is a retired educator holding a doctorate in educational studies. She served UBC's Indigenous student programs from 1989 to 2011, and received an honorary doctorate from the University of the Fraser Valley in 2011.

Richard George
Secretary / Treasurer
Ahousaht Tribe

Richard is Manager of Strategic Initiatives for Indigenous Services Canada. He holds an economics degree from Simon Fraser University and a financial management diploma from BCIT, with extensive experience in Indigenous banking and financial services, and serves on multiple community boards.

Aaron Christoff
Director
Saulteau First Nations, Northeastern BC

Aaron is a Partner at Cochrane Saxberg, Canada's largest Indigenous-led law firm, specializing in Indigenous rights, governance, and litigation. He has served on the VACFSS board since February 2018.

Bernice Albert
Director
Nlakapamux Nation, Cooks Ferry Band

Bernice holds a Bachelor's in Social Work and a Master's in Educational Administration from UBC. With a background in child counselling and teaching, she has served on the VACFSS board for seven years.

Lix Lopez
Director
Maya, Guatemala

Lix holds a Master's degree in Educational Administration from UBC and brings international experience in Indigenous peoples' education. She was initiated as a Maya Day Keeper in 2001.

How We Govern

Governance Guided by Values

The Board upholds VACFSS's commitment to belonging, respect, humility, integrity, and strength-based practice — ensuring the Society remains accountable to the children, families, and communities it serves.

Cultural Leadership
Directors bring lived Indigenous knowledge and cultural grounding to every decision.
Community Accountability
The Board ensures VACFSS remains answerable to the Nations and families it serves.
Sound Governance
Combined expertise in law, finance, education, and social work informs responsible oversight.
