About the Program

What Is the Child Safety Program?

The Child Safety Program works collaboratively with families and their First Nations community to provide culturally appropriate child safety and support services — always keeping children within their circle of family and community support where safely possible. This program is the front door for most families connecting with VACFSS for support.

When a report is received from a family, community member, or professional, our team responds with compassionate, culturally safe guidance through the child protection process — prioritizing the least disruptive measure at every step.

Our approach is grounded in restorative practice and Indigenous child welfare principles, with policies that exceed provincial legislative standards and centre each child within their circle of support.

Core Services

How the Program Supports Families

The Child Safety Program delivers three connected services — meeting families wherever they are in their journey.

Child & Family Well-Being

Works collaboratively with families and their First Nations community to provide culturally appropriate child safety and support services — keeping children within their circle of family and community support where safely possible.

  • Front door for most families connecting with VACFSS
  • Voluntary and early-intervention support
  • Connects families with culturally rooted services
Navigating Child Safety Concerns

Responds to reports from families, community members, and professionals regarding child safety concerns — offering compassionate, culturally safe guidance through the child protection process.

  • Screens and assesses incoming reports
  • Prioritizes the least disruptive measure
  • Guides families through the process step by step
Restorative & Indigenous-Led Practice

A restorative practice approach to child safety that centres children within their circle of support, with policies that exceed provincial legislative standards.

  • Emphasizes least-disruptive measures
  • Grounded in Indigenous child welfare principles
  • Keeps families together wherever safe to do so
Our Approach

Principles That Guide Every Decision

Every decision the Child Safety Program makes is grounded in these principles — keeping the child, their family, and their community at the centre.

Least Disruptive Measure

Always considering the option that keeps children connected to family first.

Family-Centred Planning

Families and community help shape every safety plan.

Cultural Safety

Indigenous child welfare principles guide every interaction.

Voice & Choice

Families' voices shape decisions that affect their children.

Continuous Improvement

Policies that exceed provincial legislative standards.

Circle of Support

Children are centred within their family and community circle.

What Happens Next

How a Concern Is Handled

We know reaching out can feel daunting. Here is what families can expect when a concern is reported.

1
Report Received

A family, community member, or professional shares a concern with our team.

2
Screening & Assessment

A worker reviews the situation with care, looking at the family's strengths and supports.

3
Family-Led Safety Planning

Families and community help build a plan using the least disruptive measure.

4
Ongoing Support

Our team stays connected with the family as they move toward stability.
