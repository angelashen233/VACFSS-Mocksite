About the Program

What Is Family Preservation & Reunification?

This program coordinates culturally-informed supports that help families heal — keeping families together while addressing safety concerns, and where children have been separated, working toward meaningful reunification.

This work draws on a family's own community, Elders, and cultural practices as sources of strength, delivered through group programs and one-to-one, in-home support.

Whether a family needs an 11-week parenting circle or one-to-one outreach counselling, the goal is the same: build the skills and connection that let children stay safely with the people who love them.

Group Programs

Cultural Teaching Circles

Group programs facilitated by respected Elders, weaving cultural teachings and traditional knowledge into parenting education and community connection.

11 Weeks
The Strengthening Families Group

Parenting education, support, and guidance — alongside support for children's social and emotional development. Facilitated by respected Indigenous Elders.

11 Weeks
The Strengthening Fathers Group

Explores parenting skills, the traditional role of fathers in Indigenous culture, and the impact of intergenerational trauma in a safe, Elder-facilitated space.

9 Weeks · Twice Annually
The Sacred Life-Givers Group

Supportive workshops for women with high-risk pregnancies, covering cultural concepts of motherhood, self-care, and community support.

In-Home & Community Support

One-to-One Support, Wherever Families Are

Alongside group programs, dedicated specialists work directly with families in their homes and communities.

PACE Program

An early intervention and educational centre supporting young children facing social, emotional, or behavioural challenges — equipping families with tools to thrive.

Watari Counselling Services

One-to-one support for high-risk youth through outreach, activity-based counselling, and advocacy around education and employment.

Family Day

A therapeutic parenting program nurturing positive parent-child relationships through hands-on coaching and structured activities.

Drake Medox Health Services

Specialized postpartum care workers supporting new families through infant care, breastfeeding, and the early weeks of parenthood.

Pacific Association of First Nations Women

Specialized home care delivered in a culturally appropriate way — supporting Indigenous community wellness through compassionate in-home services.

Big Sisters & Big Brothers

Children and youth are matched with caring adult mentors who provide consistent friendship, role modelling, and a sense of belonging.

Our Approach

Strength-Based, Culturally Rooted Healing

Every program is designed around a family's own culture, community, and capacity for growth.

Elder-Led Teaching

Cultural teachings and traditional knowledge guide every group program.

Keeping Families Together

Support is built around keeping children safely with their family wherever possible.

Meaningful Reunification

When children have been separated, the focus turns to a steady path back home.

Community as Strength

A family's own community and Elders are treated as the foundation of healing.
