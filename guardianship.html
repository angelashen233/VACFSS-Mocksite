About the Program

What Is the Guardianship Program?

The Guardianship Program manages the care and well-being of Indigenous children under Continuing Custody Orders. Our Lifelong Connections Workers focus on permanency planning, helping each child build a stable, culturally connected future.

Every plan is built around a child's identity, culture, and lifelong relationships — whether that future takes shape through adoption, extended family care, or other permanent arrangements.

Lifelong Connections Workers also help children and youth maintain meaningful relationships with their birth families, extended family, communities, and Nations — regardless of where a child ultimately calls home.

What We Do

Building Permanent, Connected Futures

Guardianship work centres on two connected goals: stability for the child today, and lifelong relationships that last well beyond their time in care.

Permanency Planning

Lifelong Connections Workers build a stable, culturally connected future for each child under a Continuing Custody Order.

  • Adoption, extended family care, or other permanent arrangements
  • Plans built around identity, culture, and relationships
Lifelong Connections

Helping children and youth maintain meaningful relationships with birth family, extended family, community, and Nation.

  • Builds foundations for a permanently connected future
  • Continues regardless of where a child calls home
Touching the Lands of Our Relations

Enables children and youth to visit their home territories, reinforcing identity and strengthening connection to the land.

  • Coordinated directly with Nations
  • Often includes time with Elders and extended family
Pathways to Permanency

Every Child's Path Looks Different

Lifelong Connections Workers consider every option, always centred on a child's identity, culture, and relationships.

Extended Family Care

Children remain connected to relatives who can offer a permanent, loving home.

Adoption

A permanent legal home, planned with the child's culture and identity at the centre.

Other Permanent Arrangements

Custom plans built around what will give each individual child the most stability.

Lifelong Cultural Ties

Connections to Nation, community, and culture continue no matter the placement.

Our Approach

Stability Today, Connection for Life

Guardianship decisions are never just about today — they're about the relationships a child will carry for the rest of their life.

Identity First

Every plan is built around the child's identity and culture.

Relationships That Last

Connections to family and Nation continue beyond placement.

Stability

Permanency planning gives children a secure foundation to grow from.

Connection to Land

Visits to home territories strengthen a child's sense of belonging.
