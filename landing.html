Open Your Home

Become a Caregiver

Caregivers provide safe, nurturing homes for Indigenous children and youth who are temporarily or permanently unable to live with their birth families. As a VACFSS caregiver, you are supported every step of the way.

  • Foster care for children needing a temporary home
  • Kinship care keeping children connected to family
  • Respite care offering planned short-term relief
  • Ongoing training, support, and financial assistance
See How It Works

Your Path to Becoming a Caregiver

We walk with you at every stage. The process typically takes 3–6 months from your first enquiry to welcoming a child into your home.

Step 1
Enquire

Contact our Caregiver Recruitment team to ask questions and express your interest.

Step 2
Assessment

A home study and background screening helps us understand your family and home environment.

Step 3
Training

Complete the PRIDE training program designed to prepare you for the rewards and challenges ahead.

Step 4
Placement

We work collaboratively to match a child whose needs align with your home and family strengths.

Step 5
Ongoing Support

Dedicated support workers, respite funding, training, and a community of caregivers are always there for you.

Youth-Led. Community-Driven.

Youth Advisory Council

The VACFSS Youth Advisory Council (YAC) is a group of young people aged 16–24 who have lived experience in the child welfare system. They advise VACFSS on services, programs, and policies — ensuring the voices of youth are heard and respected.

YAC members participate in meetings, events, and advocacy work, receiving mentorship, honorariums, and the chance to make a real difference for future generations of Indigenous youth.

Express Your Interest
Advisory Role

Share your lived experience to shape VACFSS policies, service delivery, and cultural programming that affects youth in care.

Community Events

Participate in cultural gatherings, workshops, and community events that celebrate Indigenous identity and build belonging.

Mentorship & Growth

Gain leadership skills, access mentors, earn honorariums, and build a foundation for your personal and professional future.
