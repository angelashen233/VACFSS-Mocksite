Working Together

Community Partners

Partnerships with communities are one of the foundations of our practice — building a strong circle of support around every child and family we serve.

Our Philosophy

A Circle of Support Around Every Family

We work collaboratively with affiliated First Nations and other Indigenous agencies, extended family, and community members to gather a strong circle of support around the children and families we serve. No family walks this path alone.

"Partnerships with communities are one of the foundations of our practice."

How We Partner

Ways We Work Together

Agreements With Extended Family

Formal arrangements that keep children connected to relatives and community while safety concerns are being addressed.

Voluntary Care Agreements

Partnerships with community members who step forward voluntarily to provide care for a child in need.

Case Planning Collaboration

Working directly with First Nations and families to shape culturally appropriate, community-informed care plans.

Cultural Care Plan Development

Building community-specific plans so caregivers can support a child's connection to their particular First Nation.

Our Practice Approach

Restorative Indigenous Child Welfare

Our policies are designed to exceed the standards set out in the Child, Family and Community Service Act (CFCSA). We prioritize the least disruptive measure as a vital component of restorative practice — and work to ensure every child's rights to culture, tradition, and language are upheld.

  • Policies that exceed CFCSA legislative standards
  • Least disruptive measure prioritized in every decision
  • Children's rights to culture, tradition, and language upheld
  • Community-specific care plans built in partnership with Nations
Partner With Us

Are You a First Nation or Indigenous Agency?

We invite Indigenous communities and agencies to connect with us regarding care plan development for a child from your community. Together, we can build a circle of support that honours culture, identity, and belonging.
