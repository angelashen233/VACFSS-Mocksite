Practicum Opportunities

Meaningful learning opportunities for students preparing for work in Indigenous family services — grounded in restorative practice and cultural engagement.

What We Offer

Learn Indigenous Family Services in Practice

VACFSS provides meaningful learning opportunities for students to help them prepare for work in Indigenous family services. Placements emphasize restorative practice grounded in Indigenous knowledge and cultural engagement — giving students real, hands-on experience alongside our Family Preservation, Resources, and Guardianship teams.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility Requirements

Students must be currently enrolled in one of the following programs:

BSW
Bachelor of Social Work
MSW
Master of Social Work
BCYC
Bachelor of Child and Youth Care
Timing: Students must be in their 4th year of BSW/BCYC studies, or in their practicum semester of MSW studies, to be eligible.
Who We Prioritize

Priority Candidates

VACFSS gives preference to students who meet one or more of the following:

  • Pursuing a Child Welfare or Indigenous Studies specialization
  • Identify as Indigenous
  • Pursuing a full-time block placement (full semester) rather than part-time
  • Hold a Class 5 driver's licence
Where You'll Work

Placement Departments

Students can rotate through multiple departments, plus opportunities to engage with the Youth Advisory Committee and cultural programming.

Family Preservation Resources Program Guardianship Program Youth Advisory Committee Cultural Programming
Application Process

How to Apply

Practicum placements are arranged through your school's field placement office — not by applying directly to VACFSS.

1
Contact Your Coordinator

Speak with your field placement coordinator about your interest in a VACFSS practicum.

2
Coordinator Reaches Out

Your coordinator contacts VACFSS directly at info@vacfss.com or 604.875.7000 to discuss placement availability.

3
Submit Your Materials

Submit your cover letter and resume through your coordinator for review.
