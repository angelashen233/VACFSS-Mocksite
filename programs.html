Children & Youth

Supporting Young People in Care

VACFSS is committed to the well-being of every Indigenous child and youth. Our programs prioritize cultural connection, belonging, voice, and lifelong permanency — ensuring young people grow up knowing who they are and where they come from.

  • Culturally grounded care and identity support
  • Youth voice in their own care journey
  • Connection to home territories and family
  • Employment and mentorship opportunities
Guardianship Program

Manages the care and well-being of Indigenous children under Continuing Custody Orders. Our Lifelong Connections Workers focus on permanency planning — helping each child build a stable, culturally connected future through adoption, extended family care, or other permanent arrangements.

Youth Advisory Committee (YAC)

Formed in 2010, the YAC consists of 12 Indigenous youth aged 16+ in or from VACFSS care. Members shape policy through public speaking, advocacy, and conference presentations — including internationally in Australia, Norway, and Taiwan. Two new members are selected annually.

Youth Voices

A storytelling platform where young people in and from VACFSS care share their experiences and perspectives — amplifying Indigenous youth voices to drive meaningful change in the child welfare system.

Touching the Lands of Our Relations

A cultural travel initiative enabling children and youth in care to visit their ancestral home territories — strengthening identity, belonging, and connection to the lands and Nations their families come from.

UBC Farm Program

A hands-on employment and learning opportunity for youth aged 12–15 in partnership with the UBC Institute for Aboriginal Health's Teaching and Research Garden. Youth develop skills, confidence, and a connection to the land.

Families

Walking With Families at Every Step

VACFSS works collaboratively with families, their First Nations communities, and other Indigenous agencies to provide child safety and family support rooted in cultural values. Our approach centres the child within their circle of support — using restorative, least-disruptive measures to keep families together.

Core Family Services

Child and Family Well-Being

Works collaboratively with families and their First Nations community to provide culturally appropriate child safety and support services — always keeping children within their circle of family and community support where safely possible.

Navigating Child Safety Concerns

Responds to reports from families, community members, and professionals regarding child safety concerns within Indigenous families — offering compassionate, culturally safe guidance through the child protection process.

Family Preservation & Reunification

Coordinates culturally-informed programs supporting families through their healing journey — keeping families together while addressing safety concerns, and where children have been separated, working toward meaningful reunification.

Client Concerns & Quality Assurance

Manages concerns and ensures service delivery aligns with VACFSS values of belonging, respect, humility, integrity, and strength-based practice. Every family's voice matters in shaping how we serve our community.

Group Programs

11-Week Program · Facilitated by Indigenous Elders

The Strengthening Families Group

Parenting education, support, and guidance — alongside children's social and emotional development. Sessions are facilitated by respected Indigenous Elders who weave cultural teachings throughout the program.

11-Week Program · Facilitated by Indigenous Elders

The Strengthening Fathers Group

Explores parenting skills, the traditional role of fathers in Indigenous culture, cultural teachings, parent-child interactions, and the impact of intergenerational trauma — all in a safe space facilitated by respected Elders.

9-Week Program · Offered Twice Annually

The Sacred Life-Givers Group

Supportive workshops for women with high-risk pregnancies covering cultural concepts of motherhood, self-care strategies, and community support — honouring the sacred role of Indigenous women as life-givers.

In-Home & Community Support

PACE Program

An early intervention and educational centre supporting young children facing social, emotional, or behavioural challenges — equipping families with tools to thrive.

Watari Counselling Services

One-to-one support for high-risk youth and individuals through outreach, activity-based counselling, and advocacy around education and employment.

Family Day

A therapeutic parenting program nurturing positive parent-child relationships through hands-on coaching, structured activities, and skill-building tailored to each family.

Drake Medox Health Services

Specialized postpartum care workers supporting new families through infant care, breastfeeding, and the early weeks of parenthood with cultural sensitivity.

Pacific Association of First Nations Women

Specialized home care delivered in a culturally appropriate and respectful way — supporting Indigenous community wellness through compassionate in-home services.

Big Sisters — Our Little Sisters

Girls aged 7–17 are matched with caring adult women who provide consistent mentorship, friendship, and positive role modelling — building confidence and belonging.

Big Brothers

Boys are supported by volunteer adult male mentors who build meaningful friendships through sports, hobbies, and cultural events — providing consistent positive role models.

Caregivers

Opening Your Home, Changing a Life

VACFSS caregivers provide safe, nurturing, and culturally connected homes for Indigenous children and youth. You are supported every step of the way — from your first enquiry through training, placement, and beyond.

Full Caregiver Guide

Types of Caregiving

Foster Care

Provide a temporary safe and nurturing home for a child who needs to be away from their birth family. Foster caregivers receive training, financial support, and ongoing guidance from a dedicated VACFSS support worker.

Kinship Care

Children remain connected to extended family, community, and culture through kinship caregivers — grandparents, aunties, uncles, and family friends who provide a familiar, loving environment.

Respite Care

Respite caregivers offer planned, short-term relief for primary foster families — giving caregivers the rest they need while ensuring children receive quality, consistent care.

Caregiver Support & Resources

Inclusive Foster Care (IFC) Platform

An online hub for active VACFSS caregivers with educational videos, cultural programming guides, access guidelines, care meeting support, community partner networks, and caregiver newsletters.

PRIDE Training Program

All prospective caregivers complete the PRIDE (Parent Resources for Information, Development, and Education) training — preparing them for the rewards and challenges of caring for children who have experienced trauma or disruption.

Cultural Programming

Resources and guidance helping caregivers keep children connected to their Indigenous identity, language, and culture — including access to cultural events, Elders, and community gatherings.

Indigenous Communities

Partnership Rooted in Respect

VACFSS works in deep partnership with Indigenous Nations, communities, and families — recognizing that community is the foundation of children's identity, health, and belonging. Our approach uses restorative practice to keep children connected to who they are and where they come from.

  • Nation-to-Nation collaborative planning
  • Restorative, least-disruptive practice
  • Cultural identity and language preservation
  • Community-led decision making
Collaborative Partnerships

VACFSS works Nation-to-Nation with First Nations communities to co-develop culturally appropriate care plans — ensuring children remain connected to their community, family, and heritage throughout their time in care.

Child Safety Program

A restorative practice approach to child safety that centres children within their circle of support. Emphasizing least-disruptive measures and Indigenous child welfare principles — keeping families together wherever it is safe to do so.

Lifelong Connections

Lifelong Connections Workers help children and youth in care maintain meaningful relationships with their birth families, extended family, communities, and Nations — building the foundations for a permanently connected future.

Touching the Lands of Our Relations

Enables children and youth in care to visit their home territories — reinforcing identity, strengthening connection to the land, and honouring the bonds between a child and the community they belong to.
