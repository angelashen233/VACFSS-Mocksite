About the Program

What Is the Resources Program?

The Resources Program provides safe alternate care for Indigenous children who cannot remain at home — recruiting, training, and supporting foster, kinship, and respite caregivers across the community.

Wherever possible, children remain connected to extended family, community, and culture through kinship caregivers — grandparents, aunties, uncles, and family friends who provide a familiar, loving environment.

Caregivers receive ongoing training, financial support, and guidance from a dedicated VACFSS support worker, plus access to community events and cultural programming that keeps children connected to their identity.

Types of Caregiving

How Caregivers Support Children

There are several ways caregivers provide a safe, nurturing home — each suited to a different need in a child's life.

Foster Care

A temporary, safe, and nurturing home for a child away from their birth family.

Kinship Care

Children stay connected to extended family, community, and culture through kinship caregivers.

Respite Care

Planned, short-term relief for primary foster families to prevent burnout.

Support & Resources

Supporting Caregivers Every Step of the Way

Caregivers are never on their own — the Resources Program builds a network of training, tools, and cultural connection around every placement.

PRIDE Training Program

All prospective caregivers complete PRIDE training, covering attachment, trauma-informed care, and cultural connection.

  • Required before placement
  • Prepares caregivers for trauma-informed care
Inclusive Foster Care (IFC) Platform

An online hub for active caregivers with educational videos, care meeting support, and a community partner network.

  • Keeps caregivers connected between meetings
  • Includes caregiver newsletters
Cultural Programming

Resources helping caregivers keep children connected to their Indigenous identity, language, and culture.

  • Access to cultural events and Elders
  • Community gatherings and ceremonies
Why Caregivers Choose VACFSS

What Caregivers Receive

Caregivers are supported with training, financial assistance, and a community that celebrates their commitment.

Financial Support

Ongoing financial support for the costs of caring for a child.

A Dedicated Worker

Ongoing guidance from a dedicated VACFSS support worker.

Training & Tools

PRIDE training plus access to the Inclusive Foster Care platform.

Community Events

Appreciation dinners, summer camps, and cultural gatherings.

Getting Started

How to Become a Caregiver

Becoming a foster, kinship, or respite caregiver starts with a conversation. Here's what the journey looks like.

1
Reach Out

Connect with our Resources Program team to learn what caregiving involves.

2
Home Assessment

A worker visits to understand your home, family, and capacity to care for a child.

3
Complete PRIDE Training

Prepare for the rewards and challenges of caring for children who have experienced trauma.

4
Welcome a Child

Begin your caregiving journey with ongoing support from VACFSS.
