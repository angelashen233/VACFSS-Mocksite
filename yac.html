About YAC

What Is the Youth Advisory Council?

The VACFSS Youth Advisory Council (YAC) is a group of young people aged 16–24 who have lived experience in the child welfare system. As YAC members, they advise VACFSS leadership on decisions that directly affect Indigenous youth in care.

YAC members attend regular meetings, participate in community events, and contribute to advocacy work — all while receiving meaningful support, mentorship, and honorariums for their time and knowledge.

The YAC was created because we believe that the people most affected by our services should have a voice in shaping them. Young people are experts in their own experiences, and their leadership makes VACFSS stronger.

Who Can Join

Are You Eligible?

YAC membership is open to young Indigenous people who have lived experience in the child welfare system and want to use that experience to create change.

Ages 16–24

Open to young people between 16 and 24 years of age at the time of application.

Indigenous Identity

Priority is given to First Nations, Métis, and Inuit youth with connections to Indigenous communities.

Lived Experience

You have personal experience with the child welfare system — as someone in care or a care-leaver.

Commitment to Advocacy

You want to use your voice to improve services and outcomes for youth in care.

What YAC Members Do

Three Ways to Lead

YAC members contribute in meaningful ways across three areas: advisory, community, and advocacy. Every role is valued and every voice matters.

Advisory Role

Share your lived experience to directly influence VACFSS policies, service delivery, and cultural programming.

  • Participate in monthly YAC meetings
  • Review and provide input on VACFSS programs
  • Present recommendations to leadership
  • Co-design youth-focused services
Community & Culture

Participate in cultural gatherings, workshops, and community events that celebrate Indigenous identity and build belonging.

  • Attend cultural events and ceremonies
  • Plan and lead youth gatherings
  • Connect with Elders and Knowledge Keepers
  • Support land-based learning activities
Advocacy

Represent the interests of Indigenous youth in care at the local, provincial, and national level.

  • Attend advocacy events and forums
  • Contribute to reports and submissions
  • Engage with government and policy makers
  • Share your story when and how you choose
What You Gain

Benefits of YAC Membership

Being a YAC member is about more than giving back — it's an investment in your own future, skills, and community connections.

Honorariums

Receive meaningful compensation for your time, expertise, and contributions at every meeting and event.

Mentorship

Be matched with experienced mentors from VACFSS, Indigenous leadership, and community organizations.

Skills & Training

Build leadership, public speaking, advocacy, and professional skills through hands-on opportunities.

Network

Connect with Indigenous leaders, organizations, and peers across Vancouver and BC.

Cultural Connection

Deepen your connection to culture, language, and community through YAC events and programming.

Safe Space

A trauma-informed, culturally safe environment where your story is respected and your boundaries are honoured.

Current Work

YAC Initiatives & Projects

Our current YAC members are working on initiatives that will shape VACFSS services for years to come.

Policy
Youth in Care Bill of Rights Review

YAC members are reviewing BC's Youth in Care Bill of Rights and providing recommendations to VACFSS on how to better communicate and uphold these rights for youth in our care.

Cultural
Land-Based Healing Program

Co-designing a land-based healing and cultural program for youth transitioning out of care, incorporating traditional practices, Elder guidance, and outdoor connection.

Advocacy
Aging Out of Care Resource Guide

Developing a peer-written guide to support youth transitioning out of the child welfare system at age 19, covering housing, finances, cultural supports, and community connections.

Community
Annual Youth Gathering

Planning and hosting VACFSS's annual Indigenous youth gathering — a community celebration featuring cultural activities, storytelling, sharing circles, and connection.

How to Join

Ready to Use Your Voice?

Joining the YAC is straightforward. We are here to support you through every step and answer any questions you have along the way.

1
Express Interest

Fill out the form below or email us. Let us know a little about yourself and why you want to join the YAC.

2
Connect With Us

3
Visit a Meeting

Attend a YAC meeting as a guest to get a feel for the group, meet current members, and see if it's the right fit.

4
Join the YAC

Complete a simple onboarding and become an official YAC member. Welcome to the team!
